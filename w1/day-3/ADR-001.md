# ADR-001: VictoriaMetrics vs Prometheus Federation and Elasticsearch for Time-Series Metric Storage

## Status

Accepted

## Context

As part of the AIOps architecture for anomaly detection on a payment service, we require a storage platform capable of ingesting and querying large volumes of metrics data generated by hundreds to thousands of services. The target scale ranges from 100K events/sec in the Small tier to 10M events/sec in the Large tier.

The storage layer must support:

- High ingestion throughput
- Long-term retention of metrics
- Near real-time queries for dashboards and anomaly detection
- Integration with Grafana
- Cost-efficient operation at scale

Several alternatives were evaluated:

1. Prometheus with federation
2. Elasticsearch
3. VictoriaMetrics
4. Datadog SaaS

Prometheus is widely adopted but becomes operationally complex at large scale due to federation requirements. Elasticsearch is highly flexible but resource-intensive for dense time-series workloads. Datadog provides a managed experience but introduces significant recurring subscription costs. VictoriaMetrics is a Prometheus-compatible TSDB designed specifically for high-ingestion metric workloads.

VictoriaMetrics supports the Prometheus query API and can act as a drop-in replacement for Prometheus while requiring less CPU, RAM, and storage resources than competing solutions according to VictoriaMetrics documentation. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Decision

We will use **VictoriaMetrics** as the primary time-series database for metric storage.

The final architecture is:

Payment Service → OTel Collector → Kafka → Flink → VictoriaMetrics → Grafana

VictoriaMetrics will store both raw metrics and derived anomaly-detection features generated by Flink.

## Quantified Trade-Off Analysis

Estimated monthly infrastructure costs for the Large tier (1000 services, 5 TB logs/day, 10M metric events/sec):

| Option | Estimated Monthly Cost | Typical Query Latency |
|----------|----------|----------|
| VictoriaMetrics | ~$55,000 | <1 second |
| Prometheus Federation | ~$75,000 | 1–3 seconds |
| Elasticsearch | ~$120,000 | 2–5 seconds |
| Datadog SaaS | ~$107,000 | <1 second |

These estimates are based on the cost model developed in Phase 2 and are intended for architectural comparison rather than vendor quotations.

VictoriaMetrics was selected because it provides the lowest estimated infrastructure cost while maintaining near real-time query performance.

## Consequences

### Positive

- **Lower Storage Cost:** VictoriaMetrics uses significantly less storage than competing monitoring stacks and achieves high compression ratios for time-series data. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
- **Lower Resource Consumption:** VictoriaMetrics documentation states that it generally requires less CPU, RAM, and storage than Prometheus, Thanos, Cortex, InfluxDB, and similar systems. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
- **High Scalability:** A single VictoriaMetrics instance can ingest more than 1.5 million samples per second, while the cluster version scales horizontally. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
- **Prometheus Compatibility:** Existing Grafana dashboards and PromQL queries continue to work with minimal changes. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
- **Operational Simplicity:** VictoriaMetrics can replace a federated Prometheus deployment with a simpler architecture and lower infrastructure overhead. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

### Negative

- The operations team has less experience operating VictoriaMetrics than Prometheus.
- Some third-party integrations are designed specifically for Prometheus and may require additional validation.
- The ecosystem is smaller than Prometheus and Elasticsearch.

## Alternatives Considered

### Prometheus Federation

**Pros**

- Industry standard
- Large ecosystem
- Familiar operational model

**Cons**

- Higher memory consumption
- Increased operational complexity at large scale
- Requires federation and additional infrastructure for long-term retention

**Decision:** Rejected due to higher operational overhead and infrastructure cost.

### Elasticsearch

**Pros**

- Powerful search capabilities
- Strong log analytics ecosystem

**Cons**

- High CPU, RAM, and storage requirements
- More expensive for dense metric workloads
- Increased operational complexity

**Decision:** Rejected because metrics are the primary workload rather than full-text search.

### Datadog SaaS

**Pros**

- Fully managed platform
- Minimal operational burden
- Fast deployment

**Cons**

- Recurring subscription costs increase with scale
- Reduced control over storage and retention policies

**Decision:** Rejected because estimated monthly cost at Large scale is approximately 2× higher than the self-hosted VictoriaMetrics solution.

## Conclusion

VictoriaMetrics provides the best balance of cost, scalability, operational simplicity, and performance for the anomaly-detection platform. It supports the required ingestion rate, integrates directly with Grafana, remains compatible with Prometheus tooling, and delivers lower infrastructure costs than the evaluated alternatives while maintaining sub-second query performance.
