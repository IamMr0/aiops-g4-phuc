# ADR-001: VictoriaMetrics vs Elasticsearch/Prometheus for Time-Series Metric Storage

## Status
Accepted

## Context
As part of our AIOps architecture for Anomaly Detection on the Payment Service, we need a highly scalable storage layer capable of ingesting and querying massive volumes of metrics data. The system must support scale tiers ranging from 100K events/sec up to 10M events/sec. 

Traditionally, Prometheus is used for metrics and Elasticsearch for logs. However, self-hosting Prometheus at 10M events/sec requires complex federation and significant RAM/CPU overhead. Elasticsearch is highly versatile but is famously resource-heavy for dense time-series metrics, requiring huge heaps and expensive SSD arrays to maintain acceptable query latency.

We need a solution that minimizes storage costs (specifically hot storage) while retaining sub-second query latencies for Flink processing and Grafana dashboards.

## Decision
We will use **VictoriaMetrics** as the primary Time-Series Database (TSDB) for all metric data instead of Prometheus or Elasticsearch.

## Consequences
### Positive
- **Cost Reduction:** VictoriaMetrics achieves superior data compression compared to Prometheus and Elasticsearch. We estimate a 50-70% reduction in storage volume, which directly reduces hot storage (SSD) costs at the Large tier (10M events/sec).
- **Compute Efficiency:** It is optimized for low CPU and RAM footprint. We can ingest millions of data points per second on a single node or a small cluster, greatly reducing EC2 instance compute costs.
- **Compatibility:** It is fully compatible with PromQL, meaning our Grafana dashboards and any existing Prometheus-based alerting will work out of the box without requiring query rewrites.

### Negative
- **Operational Familiarity:** The DevOps team has extensive experience with Elasticsearch and Prometheus, but less operational knowledge of VictoriaMetrics cluster management. 
- **Tooling:** While PromQL is supported, some edge-case Prometheus remote-write features or ecosystem plugins might have limited support or require workarounds compared to native Prometheus.

## Alternatives Considered
- **Elasticsearch:** Rejected due to excessive compute and storage costs at 10M events/sec. Handling dense metrics in Lucene indices leads to bloated storage size and memory pressure.
- **Prometheus (Federated):** Rejected due to operational complexity at massive scale. Managing Prometheus federation for 1000 services and 10M eps requires significant engineering effort and higher resource overhead. 
- **Datadog (SaaS):** Rejected for cost reasons. At 10M events/sec, SaaS metric pricing scales linearly and becomes prohibitively expensive (estimated ~$500k/month), making a self-hosted VictoriaMetrics cluster vastly more economical despite the operational overhead.
